# From Junior to Senior: The Non-Technical Path

The gap between junior and senior developer isn't just technical skills.

## Communication is Code

A senior developer writes documentation, explains trade-offs, and communicates constraints. Your ability to explain is as important as your ability to code.

## Ownership Mentality

Don't just complete tickets. Understand the business context, ask "why?", and take responsibility for outcomes.

## Navigating Ambiguity

Juniors need clear specs. Seniors thrive in ambiguity — they break down vague requirements into actionable plans.

The path is non-linear, but every step shapes the engineer you become.
